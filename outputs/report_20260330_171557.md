## Executive Summary

This report explores the CRISPR-Cas9 system, focusing on its key components, mechanisms for gene editing, medical applications, and ethical considerations. The findings highlight the system's targeted approach to genetic modifications and its implications in medicine.

## What are the key components of the CRISPR-Cas9 system?

- CRISPR is a genetic engineering technique that enables specific targeted changes in an organism's DNA. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_editing)
- CRISPR gene editing is categorized as SDN (Site-Directed Nucleases), which targets a specific part of the genome. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_editing)

## How does the CRISPR-Cas9 mechanism facilitate gene editing?

- SDN1 makes random mutations at the target site to repair damaged host DNA without involving foreign DNA. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_editing)
- SDN2 uses small non-coding homologous repair DNA to achieve a specific nucleotide sequence to repair host DNA. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_editing)
- SDN3 uses a large stretch of protein coding donor DNA targeted for insertion through HDR (Homology-Directed Repair) at a predefined genomic locus. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_editing)
